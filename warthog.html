WARTHOG

Warthogs (Phacochoerus africanus) are wild pigs that are native to Africa. They are known for their distinctive appearance, with large tusks and a rugged, bristly coat. Warthogs are well adapted to their environment, able to survive in a variety of habitats, including savannas and grasslands.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

You will find the warthog at pen G18, near the savannah habitat zone.

WHAT DO THEY EAT?

Warthogs are herbivores and primarily feed on grasses, roots, and tubers. They are known to forage on a variety of vegetation, including acacia trees and other savannah plants.
